## Calibration

1. **Weakest finding.** The I18n placeholder-replacement bug at `herramienta/I18n.java:628-629` is the weakest: I argued `String.replace(CharSequence, CharSequence)` is non-replacing per replacement of the *substring*, but `String.replace` actually does a literal-replace of the *first* occurrence and returns a new `String` — the issue is more subtle (the inner `replace` re-scans the *new* `raw` on the next iteration and may inject placeholders that weren't in the input). The behavior is also bounded by the fact that `args` values today come from numeric/format outputs, not user input. Stronger evidence would be a unit test demonstrating the corruption with a realistic arg, plus a count of call sites in `presentacio/` that pass user-derived strings to `I18n.t`.
2. **New class to look for next run.** I want to systematically check **public mutability**: the pattern of `public static Color` / `public static Font` / `public final List` fields in `herramienta.UITheme`, `interficie.LectorBibliotecaEnMemoria`, `Configuracio` — these all leak state through fields that should be encapsulated behind accessors (or records), and the write/read threading invariants are undocumented.
3. **confidence: 75.** I verified every CRITICAL/HIGH finding against the source (Llista equals/hashCode, DelegatPrestatgeria missing lock, ClientOpenLibrary connection leaks, Configuracio char[]→String, UITheme public mutable statics, Executable silent bootstrap failure, EscritorBiblioteca duplicate declarations, ControladorMostrarBiblioteca JDBC-on-EDT). MEDIUM findings are pattern-based and some LOW ones (e.g. `ConfiguracioDataSection` style duplication) are taste. The dominant risk to the confidence bar is the presentacio JDBC-on-EDT cluster — verified the pattern in `ControladorMostrarBiblioteca.java:164,170` directly, but only spot-checked the rest; some files the subagent reported may be one-shot writes where the EDT hit is genuinely cheap.
